Welcome to Delugeo!

Flooding is an ever-growing global concern, particularly in the UK. England sustains around £1.5 billion of damage each year from flooding, directly or indirectly, according to a 2015 report by Sayers and Partners[1], with 1.4 million people living in houses with a 12.5% likelihood of being flooded during a 10-year period[2].

Use Delugeo to explore UK flooding vulnerability from five perspectives: the global, the natural, the social, the infrastructural, and the personal.

How does the UK fit into the global context?

Global Overview

To situate flooding in the UK into the global context, the first visualisation provides a global overview of large flood events occuring since 1985 based on data from Dartmouth Flood Observatory’s active archive[4]. The archive constitutes the most extensive database of large flood events with a global coverage, even though some information might be inaccurate due to the fact a large share of the information comes from news sources. Large flooding events are defined as those inflicting significant damage to structures or agriculture or causing fatalities. The visualisation allows users to see the global flooding trends, as well as to zoom in and explore specific flooding events in more detail.

Why is the UK so exposed?

Increasing Rainfall

According to Sir James Bevan, Chief Executive of the Environment Agency[5], high levels of exposure to surface-water flooding in the UK is the result of at least three factors. First, an increasing population, and so more people at risk. Second, an increasing proportion of surfaces covered in impermeable concrete. Thirdly, an increase in rainfall. There has been a notable increase in the number of daily rainfall totals exceeding 50mm since 1960, with a 17% increase in extremely wet days between the periods 1960-1990 and 2008-2017[6].

Use this tool to explore the trends of extreme rainfall at the regional scale of North Wales over the last 80 years. Note that whilst there is large variation in the trends for individual month, there is a noticeable increase in extreme rainfall events when all months are considered. Though the increase is gradual, the MET office anticipates that rainfall will like continue to steadily increase due to global warming and put an increasing pressure on society, demanding ever increasing funding, precautions, and anticipation.

How is the UK population impacted by these floods?

Population Vulnerability

How badly people will be affected will depend not just on their exposure to the floods, but on their social vulnerability – that is, how well they are able to cope with and respond to events. Social vulnerability relates to[7]:

  • Personal features of the individual, which affect sensitivity to climate impacts
  • Environmental characteristics, which can increase or offset exposure to flooding or heat
  • Social and institutional context, which affect people’s ability to adapt
The map plots the indices of different social aspects of vulnerability to floods in various output areas in the UK, for analyzing the source of population vulnerability. Urban areas and population density are provided as additional insights. Categorical indices represent different coping abilities in the face of floods, thus reflecting in what way and to what extent a local area is socially vulnerable.

How is the UK responding to this risk?

Local Flood Defenses

In London, 1.25 million people live and work in areas with flood risk from river and sea. Another 165,000 people are at medium risk of flooding from rainfall. Because London is located in the low-lying plain area of the river mouth, it is threatened by multiple floods from rivers, oceans and rainstorms. It has experienced many major floods in history, and the flood control pressure of the city is also on the rise in the future. The London flood defence system was established in 1982, and has been continuously upgraded and expanded until now. The visualization presents data about London historical flood, the at flood risk areas and all flood defense facilities.

Am I at Risk?

Flood Risk AR

The augmented reality (AR) web application shows which flood risk zone the current location is in. Acquiring GPS location information from your smartphone, the app allows you to interactively check the flood zone, flood alert and flooded history of the locations around you. For flood zones, Very Low means less than 0.1% of flood probability for a year; Low: 0.1-1%; Medium: 1-3.3%; High: more than 3.3%. The app performs well in smartphone uses both of iOS and Android OS.
